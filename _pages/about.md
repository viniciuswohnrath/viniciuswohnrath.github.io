I am a sociologist of education currently affiliated with the Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México (UNAM), where I conduct postdoctoral research in the history and sociology of education.

My research agenda examines political socialization, intellectual formation, religion and politics, and disputes over legitimacy in Latin America. I work at the intersection of sociology of education, historical sociology, political sociology, and socio-historical approaches to law and the state.

My current ethnographic research investigates the political formation of indigenous teachers and the appropriation of critical pedagogical traditions at the Escuela Normal Indígena de Michoacán, in Cherán K'eri (Mexico), an autonomous indigenous community marked by intense territorial conflict and political mobilization.

Previously, I conducted research at the University of Campinas (Unicamp), the École des Hautes Études en Sciences Sociales (EHESS, Paris), and the École Normale Supérieure Paris-Saclay (ENS Paris-Saclay), examining legal elites, Catholic intellectuals, constitutional processes, and the institutionalization of rights in Brazil through socio-historical and prosopographical approaches.
